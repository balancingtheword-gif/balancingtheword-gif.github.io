Balancing the Word Ministry

What We Teach

This statement summarizes the foundational doctrines taught by Balancing the Word Ministry. We encourage every believer to examine the Scriptures prayerfully and to remain grounded in the whole counsel of God.

The Scriptures

The Bible

We teach that the sixty-six books of the Holy Scriptures comprise one book: the Bible. These Scriptures are the tangible Word of God, written for the guidance of humankind unto righteousness. The answers to many of life's questions are found in the biblical Scriptures (Proverbs 6:23).

The Holy Scriptures are alive, relevant today, and without error (Hebrews 4:12; 6:17-18). They are forever established (Isaiah 40:8), will never pass away (Matthew 24:35), and are the true Word of YHWH (John 17:17). They enlighten us, guide our journey of faith (Psalm 119:105; 18:28), expose the darkness of this world, and empower us to escape temptation (1 Corinthians 10:13).

Through the Holy Scriptures, YHWH reveals Himself to humankind as Sovereign God, Creator, Father, Redeemer, and Judge, so that all may know the way of salvation (Romans 16:26). The Bible contains YHWH's promises (2 Peter 1:4) and His prophecies to prepare us for what is to come (Amos 3:7). Although Yahweh is the Supreme Author, He moved men through the power of His Holy Spirit to write His Word for the fulfillment of His purposes (2 Timothy 3:16-17).

We encourage all believers in the Lord Jesus Christ to read and study the Bible daily and to submit themselves to sound biblical teaching.

The Trinity

The Father, the Son, and the Holy Spirit

We teach that God the Father, Jesus the Son, and the Holy Spirit together form the Trinity. Each is distinct in person and position, yet eternal and identical in divine nature. They move and work in perfect unity and oneness to bring about the promises and purposes of sovereign Yahweh. Each member of the Trinity is worthy to be honored and worshiped (2 Corinthians 13:14).

Yahweh the Father and Jesus the Son are one (John 10:30), and Yahweh has given Jesus all authority in heaven and on earth (Matthew 28:18). The Holy Spirit, the Spirit of Yahweh, aids believers in every aspect of life and enables us in our journey of faith (John 14:26).

Yahweh

We teach that God has many names, but we refer to Him as YHWH, or Yahweh, translated from the Hebraic meaning "I AM" (Exodus 3:14). He is the Creator and Father of all that exists: the heavens, the earth, and all that is in them (Isaiah 42:5). Yahweh is the only true living God (Jeremiah 10:10) and is sovereign over all creation, performing His will (Psalm 115:3).

Yahweh is Spirit (John 4:24) and love (1 John 4:8). He is all-knowing (Hebrews 4:13), all-present (Proverbs 15:3), and all-powerful (Daniel 4:35). As the Supreme Creator and Father, He is also the righteous Judge who will execute the final judgment (Revelation 20:13).

Jesus

We teach that Jesus is the only begotten Son of Yahweh (John 3:16). He came from the lineage of David (Revelation 22:16), was conceived by the Holy Spirit (Luke 1:35), and was born of a virgin woman, sharing in humanity (Galatians 4:4). He possessed the physical appearance and attributes of a man and experienced human needs and emotions (Philippians 2:5-7). He was born under the Mosaic Law, and His name was established accordingly (Luke 2:21-22).

Jesus is fully Yahweh, possessing the complete divine nature of the Trinity (Colossians 2:9), and He is the Word of Yahweh made in the likeness of humankind (John 1:14). He fulfilled the promise of the New Covenant (Jeremiah 31:31; Hebrews 7:22) and satisfied the Law and the Prophets (Matthew 5:17).

Jesus proclaimed repentance, taught all nations the pathway to salvation, and demonstrated mercy through many miracles by the power of the Holy Spirit (Matthew 4:17, 23). He came to do the Father's will (John 6:38), freely offering Himself as the pure, righteous, and final sacrifice to redeem the world from sin (Hebrews 10:10).

We teach that Jesus was scourged and crucified according to Roman tradition (Luke 18:32-33), rose from the dead on the third day (1 Corinthians 15:4), and now sits at the right hand of the Father (Mark 16:19). Our faith rests in the resurrection of Jesus Christ (1 Peter 1:3).

The Holy Spirit

We teach that the Holy Spirit, as a member of the Trinity, embodies the fullness of Yahweh's divine nature (Genesis 1:2). He is a promise (Joel 2:28), a gift (Acts 2:38), and a Helper (John 14:26). He reminds us of the Scriptures, guides us into the will of Yahweh, transforms us to be like Christ, anoints us, comforts us, strengthens us in weakness, and works in many other ways (Ephesians 3:16).

The Holy Spirit gives spiritual gifts that empower believers to reveal Yahweh's glory to the world. He anoints us to fulfill our place in the body of Christ (1 Corinthians 12:8-10, 28), produces His fruit in us (Galatians 5:22-23), gives us power to testify in His name (Acts 1:8), and reveals Yahweh's greatness (Mark 16:17-18). Yet we can resist Him (Acts 7:51), grieve Him (Ephesians 4:30), quench Him (1 Thessalonians 5:19), and commit blasphemy against Him, which is the unforgivable sin (Mark 3:29).

Humankind

Adam and Mankind

We teach that Yahweh created male and female in His image, blessing them to reproduce and subdue the earth (Genesis 1:27-28). He created them a little lower than Himself, perfect, without sin, and in right fellowship with Him (Psalm 8:5).

We teach that Yahweh created humankind with free will to make decisions (Deuteronomy 30:19). Yet Adam disobeyed Yahweh, causing sin and death to permeate the world (Romans 5:12) and resulting in humanity being born with a sinful nature (Psalm 51:5). As a result, all have fallen short of His righteousness (Romans 3:10, 23) and are deserving of Yahweh's wrath and eternal torment (Ephesians 2:3).

Salvation and the Need

Salvation

We teach that salvation is the only hope for humankind's deliverance from Yahweh's wrath and eternal death. Only through Yahweh's undeserved gift of grace, His only begotten Son, Jesus Christ, can we receive salvation (Titus 2:11).

We teach that only through believing in Jesus, the only begotten Son of Yahweh, can we have redemption and salvation unto eternal life (Acts 4:12; John 3:16). Salvation is received by grace through faith, not by good works, behavior, or religious affiliation (Ephesians 2:8-9).

We teach that sincere faith reveals Yahweh's love and produces sorrowful repentance from unrighteousness, resulting in salvation (2 Corinthians 7:10). Through salvation, we are set free from the bondage of sin, darkness, and eternal torment (Romans 8:2) and are translated into His kingdom of eternal life (Colossians 1:13). We become fellow heirs with Christ, members of His body (Romans 8:17; 12:5), and children of Yahweh (John 1:12).

The Need

We teach that the fallen nature of humankind results in enslavement to sin, rebellion against Yahweh, and a life of wickedness and lust (2 Timothy 2:26), leading to eternal death (Romans 6:23). For this reason, humankind needs salvation for redemption and restoration into right fellowship with Yahweh, freedom from sin's bondage, and rescue from eternal death (Matthew 25:41). Through salvation, Jesus transforms humankind into His likeness and restores us according to His eternal purpose (Philippians 3:20-21).

The Church

The Way

We teach that all believers belong to The Way as one body, His Church. Anyone who believes in the heart that Jesus is the only begotten Son of Yahweh (John 3:16), that He was put to death for our sins (Romans 5:8), that He was resurrected (1 Peter 1:3), and who confesses that Jesus is Lord, is a member of His body (Romans 10:9; 12:4-5).

All believers are growing and being transformed into one Church, with Jesus as our foundation, so that we may be the dwelling place of the Holy Spirit (Ephesians 2:19-22). All believers are His Church, "made without hands," belonging to The Way (Acts 7:48; 24:14). Therefore, wherever we go and gather, we are Yahweh's Church.

Organization of the Church

We teach the Scriptures that instruct us in the proper organization of the Church. Yahweh appoints apostles, prophets, teachers, spiritual gifts, and ministries among the members (1 Corinthians 12:28). Wherever believers gather, proper order should be in place for the flow of the Holy Spirit, the teaching of Scripture, ministry to one another, and unity among the gathered believers.

Overseers, Deacons, Deaconesses, and Elders

We teach that an overseer is an administrator who manages the affairs of the Church and helps ensure that it functions well and meets the needs of the members. The overseer is not the pastor or the only leader of the Church. This office carries significant responsibility, and whoever seeks it must meet the scriptural requirements (1 Timothy 3:1-7).

We teach that men and women can serve as deacons and deaconesses. They serve, shepherd, attend, teach, and fulfill the tasks of the Church, meeting the scriptural requirements for service (1 Timothy 3:8-13).

We teach that elders are spiritually mature, wise, knowledgeable in the Scriptures, and beyond reproach. Elders may be appointed by the overseer or by believers within the community or city, and an elder may also be appointed as an overseer (Titus 1:5-9). Their duties include preaching, teaching (1 Timothy 5:17), praying for the sick (James 5:14-15), and discipling the young (1 Peter 5:5). We advocate that elders remain active in the community, in the Church, and in ministry to fellow believers. They are worthy of honor and respect.

Members

We encourage members to exercise their spiritual gifts and ministries freely (1 Corinthians 12:4-11). We teach discipleship and mentoring for new believers by those mature in the Lord so that each believer may find their place in the body, grow confident in the Lord, and testify of Jesus to the world.

We endorse the fivefold ministries and believe each office currently exists and will continue to exist (Ephesians 4:11). We welcome believers who are biblically aligned and doctrinally sound to exercise their gifts and ministries.

We teach believers to conduct themselves as children of light, revealing goodness, righteousness, and truth (Ephesians 5:8-10). We are to love one another (John 13:34), love our neighbors (Romans 13:10), and pursue peace with all people (Hebrews 12:14).

We teach and admonish believers to set time aside each day for prayer, reading, and studying the Scriptures. As believers grow in relationship with the Lord, they are gradually transformed into His likeness (Romans 12:2) and gain knowledge, wisdom, and confidence to handle His Word accurately (2 Timothy 2:15).

We teach that believers should care for their well-being in every area of life (Romans 12:1), be good stewards over the blessings of Yahweh (1 Peter 4:10), dress modestly as His representatives (1 Timothy 2:9), walk in wisdom (Colossians 4:5), engage in good deeds (Titus 3:8), cease from immorality (1 Thessalonians 5:21-22), strengthen one another, and present the Gospel by word and deed (Colossians 3:16-17).

Supporting the Saints

We teach the New Covenant doctrine of giving from the heart during times of distress among fellow believers and to those who diligently work in preaching, teaching, and serving others in the name of Christ (3 John 1:8). Giving should be done freely and with joy (2 Corinthians 9:7). We teach believers to give whatever is needed, such as money, food, clothing, and other material support, so that we may strengthen and aid one another in our journey of faith.

Commemorations

Baptisms and Communion

We teach that the Scriptures mention three baptisms, yet they are all under one baptism in the fullness of the Lord Jesus Christ (Ephesians 4:5):

  1. Baptism in the name of the Lord Jesus, the baptism of salvation (Acts 8:16).
  2. Baptism of repentance for the forgiveness of sin, water baptism (Matthew 3:11a).
  3. Baptism of the Holy Spirit, given by the Lord (Mark 1:8).

We teach that when a sinner believes the Gospel of Jesus Christ and repents from unrighteousness, they are baptized in repentance for the forgiveness of sins and in the name of the Lord Jesus Christ, resulting in salvation. We also teach that a new convert may immediately receive the separate baptism of the Holy Spirit, endowing them with power. In this way, the believer receives the fullness of Yahweh as one baptism.

Water Baptism

We teach that water baptism has a twofold purpose: it testifies to the new convert's repentance unto forgiveness of sin and acceptance of Jesus Christ as Savior (Romans 6:4), and it consecrates believers as priests (Exodus 29:4).

We teach that water baptism is not required for total salvation (Luke 23:42-43) or membership in the body of Christ (1 Corinthians 1:17). Once a person receives the gift of grace, Jesus Christ, they are immediately saved and are members of His body.

According to Scripture, the requirement for baptism is to believe in the Gospel of Jesus Christ with all one's heart (Acts 16:33). Any disciple of Christ is qualified to conduct baptisms (John 4:1-2), though we firmly encourage the safe conduct of water baptisms.

We teach that water baptism is the total immersion of the convert into water. However, in challenging circumstances, whatever is possible to experience baptism is acceptable. We strongly encourage converts to participate in this joyful commemoration as a testimony to their new life (2 Corinthians 5:17). Water baptism also testifies that believers are consecrated as priests to proclaim the Gospel of Jesus Christ throughout the world (1 Peter 2:9).

Holy Spirit Baptism

We teach that the baptism of the Holy Spirit is a separate experience from salvation (Acts 19:4-6). Similar to water baptism, the believer is immersed by being filled with the outpouring of the Spirit (Acts 2:1-4). Any believer may receive the baptism of the Holy Spirit if they desire (Acts 5:32).

We teach that believers should receive the baptism of the Holy Spirit so they may have power in faith to be His witnesses throughout the world (Acts 1:8). Once a believer receives the baptism of the Holy Spirit, they are endowed with spiritual gifts, ministry gifts (1 Corinthians 12:8-10, 28), and the fruit of the Spirit (Galatians 5:22-23).

Communion

We teach that Communion is partaken in remembrance of Jesus, His sacrifice, and His New Covenant. The elements usually consist of unleavened bread, wine, or grape juice, representing His broken body and His shed blood (Luke 22:19-20). Communion should be conducted prayerfully, orderly, and reverently while examining ourselves. It may be taken at any time and as often as desired, individually or with a group (1 Corinthians 11:25-29).

We teach that believers should partake in Communion regularly, yet it is not a requirement for salvation. We also teach that all believers are priests and may conduct a Communion service. Communion is a matter of the heart and not merely of tradition; therefore, if typical elements are unavailable, whatever is available, such as bread and water, is sufficient.

The Biblical Family

The Family

We teach that the family is the most essential and sacred of Yahweh's creations. It builds communities, nations, and influences the world. The family derives from a holy union, or matrimony, between one biological male and one biological female (Genesis 2:23-24). This union is sealed by Yahweh and legally recognized by the government, a church, and a community, providing protections for spouses and children (Proverbs 13:22a).

Marriage places the believer in obedience to the Lord's command to multiply and fill the earth (Genesis 1:28) by producing children (Psalm 127:3). We teach that not all are called to marriage, yet if one desires marriage and family, then for the sake of purity we strongly encourage holy union (1 Corinthians 7:7-9).

The Order

We teach that the ordained order of the family is: "Yahweh is the head of Christ, Christ is the head of every man, and the husband is the head of the wife" (1 Corinthians 11:3).

Husbands, Wives, and Children

We teach that the husband is to submit to Christ first to gain faith and wisdom in caring for his family. He is to love his wife, hold her in high esteem, cherish, honor, and cleave to her (Ephesians 5:25-31). He is to be kind and understanding toward her and treat her as a fellow heir of Jesus Christ (1 Peter 3:7). As the head of the family under Christ, he is to provide for and manage his household well (1 Timothy 5:8) and raise his children in the instruction and discipline of the Lord (Ephesians 6:4).

We teach that a wife who honors the Lord is priceless, and her husband has confidence in her. She manages her household well, teaches her family with wisdom and kindness, ministers to the unfortunate, and looks joyfully to the future (Proverbs 31:10, 11, 20, 25-27). We teach that the wife is to submit only to her husband and respect him as the head under Christ and as the provider and protector of the family (Ephesians 5:22, 33).

We teach that children are to honor and obey their parents (Ephesians 6:1-3) by adhering to their instruction, teaching, and discipline (Proverbs 1:8; 13:1). In this manner, they will be pleasing to the Lord (Colossians 3:20).

Divorce

We strongly encourage the family to remain intact whenever possible. However, we acknowledge that we live in difficult times, and God's endless grace and mercy cover us (1 Corinthians 7:15).

The Nation of Israel

Abraham

We teach that Yahweh chose Abraham from among all the people of the world and made a covenant with him to become a great nation (Genesis 12:1-3). Abraham's descendants are God's chosen nation, Israel (Deuteronomy 7:6).

We teach that from Israel the Gospel of Jesus Christ spread throughout the world (Luke 24:47), fulfilling Yahweh's promise to Abraham that through his seed all the families of the earth would be blessed (Genesis 28:14). Therefore, as believers in the faith, we are blessed through Abraham (Galatians 3:9), and it is our duty to pray for and bless Israel (Psalm 122:6).

The Evil One

Lucifer and Satan

We teach that Lucifer was a beautiful angel created by Yahweh and was a covering cherub (Ezekiel 28:13-15). However, he became prideful and revolted against Yahweh, seeking to take His throne. Yahweh expelled him from the heavens (Isaiah 14:11-15). From the moment of Lucifer's fall, he became a relentless opponent of Yahweh, whom we now call Satan.

We teach that Satan is the ruler of the world (John 14:30), the prince of the air (Ephesians 2:2), the tempter (Matthew 4:3), and the accuser of the children of Yahweh (Revelation 12:10). As followers of Christ, we are to remain alert and resist him by faith (1 Peter 5:8-9).

Victory

We teach that Jesus has given believers authority over the power of the Evil One (Luke 10:19) and that by faith we can overcome this world (1 John 5:4). We have victory by abiding in His Word (1 John 2:14), by prayer (Luke 22:40), by doing good (Romans 12:21), and by resisting the devil (James 4:7). God rescues His people (2 Peter 2:9) and provides a way of escape (1 Corinthians 10:13).

Eschatology

The Last Days and the Great Tribulation

We teach that the New Testament describes events and signs that forewarn us of the last days. Because many current events align with Scripture, we teach that our modern era is experiencing the last days. We accept that these events must take place for Yahweh to fulfill His Word and prepare the way for the second coming of Jesus (Matthew 24:8, 12, 14).

We teach that the sign of the beginning of the Great Tribulation is the Abomination of Desolation, when the Antichrist sets his abode within the temple in Jerusalem and establishes his power, corrupting its sanctity. During this time, many will suffer severe affliction and persecution unlike any time before or after. Yet, because of Yahweh's grace, those days will be cut short for the sake of believers in Christ (Matthew 24:15, 21-22). A remnant of Christ's believers will survive the Tribulation and worship before His throne (Revelation 7:14-15).

The Second Coming and the Rapture

We teach that when Christ comes again, He will come in great power, and every person will see Him (Matthew 24:30). He will come as King of kings and Lord of lords, overcoming the Antichrist and his followers (Revelation 1:7; 17:14).

We teach that the rapture is when all believers in Jesus, whether dead or alive, will arise and be changed into imperishable beings (1 Corinthians 15:51-52). The members of His body, who make up His bride, will be caught up together to meet Him in the clouds and will forever be with Him (1 Thessalonians 4:16-17). No one knows when that time will come, so we teach that believers must remain alert and in communion with our Lord so we will hear the trumpet sound (Matthew 24:36).

The Final Judgment

We teach that all humankind, dead and alive, whether great or small, will stand before the mighty throne of Yahweh and receive His final judgment according to their deeds. Anyone whose name is not written in the Book of Life will suffer the second and eternal death. Those whose names are found in the Book of Life, His bond-servants, will inherit eternal life and serve Him (Revelation 20:11-15; 22:3-4).

We encourage every reader to search the Scriptures, pray for discernment, and grow in faithful obedience to Jesus Christ.
