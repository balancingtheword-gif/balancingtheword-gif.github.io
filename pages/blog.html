Answering the Call: Ambassadors of Christ

The Call to True Discipleship

By Lesli D. Mize

Published May 20, 2026

A call to true discipleship, faithful obedience, and Kingdom-centered living through surrender to Christ rather than the pursuit of earthly platforms or personal empires.

Answering the Call: Ambassadors of Christ is a powerful call to wholehearted surrender and true discipleship in Jesus Christ.

Jesus never called people to casual belief, cultural Christianity, or comfortable religion. He did not come seeking followers who merely acknowledged Him intellectually while refusing to surrender fully to His authority. Nor did He call people to identify with Christianity simply as a tradition, social identity, or as an outward label without experiencing true transformation of heart and life. Jesus also never offered a faith built around comfort, convenience, or personal ease in other word, into a religion that avoids sacrifice, repentance, obedience, and the daily carrying of the cross. Instead, He called men and women to deny themselves, surrender completely to His lordship, and follow Him as true disciples of the Kingdom. He never lowered the standard to gain followers, but continually called believers to faithful obedience, wholehearted devotion, and lives fully yielded to Him.

This is because the Kingdom of God has never been built through superficial belief, outward religion, or human ambition. From the very beginning, Jesus made clear that His Church and His Kingdom would be established by His authority alone through surrendered disciples who faithfully follow Him.

So, Jesus Himself settled the matter:

“I will build My church.”

Matthew 16:18

Only Christ alone is the Builder, and Christ alone is the King.

If God alone builds His Kingdom, then the role of the believer must be rightly understood. Scripture never commands the Church to establish God’s Kingdom through worldly power, religious ambition, or human influence. That authority only belongs to Christ alone.

The Great Commission

Instead, Jesus gave His followers a very different commission:

“Go therefore and make disciples of all nations, baptizing them in the name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit, teaching them to observe all things that I have commanded you…”

Matthew 28:19–20

This command reveals how God advances His Kingdom on earth. Jesus did not instruct His followers to build earthly empires, pursue worldly influence, or establish monuments to themselves. He commanded them to make disciples.

The phrase “all nations” does not merely refer to political countries or geographic borders. It speaks of all peoples—all ethnicities, tribes, languages, and cultures. The gospel was never intended for one group alone, but for the entire world. The Kingdom of God transcends every earthly boundary, calling people from every nation into allegiance to Christ the King.

Jesus also commanded His disciples to teach others “to observe all things” that He had commanded. This was never meant to be mere intellectual instruction or religious information. True discipleship involves teaching others to live according to the example, truth, and commands of Christ Himself. The disciples were called to pass on what they had personally seen, heard, learned, and observed while walking with Jesus. In the same way, believers today are called not only to proclaim truth with their lips, but to model lives transformed by obedience to Him.

Baptism and Consecration

Christ further commanded that believers be baptized in the name of the Father, the Son, and the Holy Spirit. Baptism is more than a religious ceremony; it is an outward declaration of inward surrender. It identifies the believer with Christ’s death, burial, and resurrection and publicly declares a new allegiance to the Kingdom of God. Through baptism, the disciple openly testifies that the old life has been left behind and that they now belong to Jesus Christ.

In many ways, baptism also reflects the washing and consecration of the priests in the Old Testament before they entered into service before the Lord. Just as the priests were washed and set apart for holy service unto God, baptism signifies that the believer has been cleansed, consecrated, and set apart unto Christ as part of a royal priesthood sent into the world. Scripture declares that believers are “a royal priesthood” (1 Peter 2:9), called to represent God among the nations and proclaim His truth to the world. Baptism, therefore, is not merely symbolic of personal faith, but also identifies the disciple as one who now belongs to the Kingdom and has been commissioned to serve faithfully under the authority of the King.

Bowing Before the King

This is how God’s Kingdom advances on earth—not through monuments, personalities, or earthly empires, but through transformed lives surrendered to Jesus Christ. The Kingdom becomes visible wherever men and women bow before the authority of the King.

To bow before Christ’s authority means more than simply acknowledging that He is Lord. It means submitting every area of life to His rule—our desires, decisions, priorities, relationships, ambitions, and will. It is the recognition that Jesus does not merely offer guidance; He possesses rightful authority over those who belong to Him. To fully yield to Christ is to lay down personal control and willingly obey His Word, even when obedience is difficult, costly, or contrary to the values of the world.

True surrender is not passive. It requires continual transformation through prayer, obedience, and faithful devotion to God’s truth. This is why the frequent reading, study, and meditation upon the Scriptures are essential to true discipleship. Through God’s Word, believers gain wisdom, understanding, discernment, and a clearer knowledge of His will. Scripture renews the mind, exposes sin, strengthens faith, and teaches the disciple how to walk in obedience before the Lord. A disciple cannot faithfully follow Christ while remaining disconnected from the very Word through which Christ reveals Himself.

True discipleship is not merely attending church, claiming belief, or carrying religious language; rather, it is a life fully yielded to Christ. It is a daily surrender to His authority, a continual pursuit of His will, and a steadfast commitment to follow Him wherever He leads. Anything less is imitation.

A Kingdom Not of This World

Jesus declared plainly:

“My kingdom is not of this world.”

John 18:36

The Kingdom of God does not originate from earthly systems, nor does human power sustain it. Long before Christ walked the earth, Daniel prophesied:

“The God of heaven will set up a kingdom which shall never be destroyed.”

Daniel 2:44

Christ does not require political authority to reign, nor does He need cultural acceptance to remain King. Moreover, His rule is eternal, sovereign, and unchallenged.

When believers pray,

“Your kingdom come, Your will be done, on earth as it is in heaven” (Matthew 6:10)—we are not asking humanity to build the Kingdom through human effort. Rather, we are longing for the will of God to be fully revealed and ultimately fulfilled through the return of Christ.

God’s Will Revealed Through Scripture

God’s will is not hidden in mystery or left for humanity to define according to personal desire, cultural opinion, or worldly wisdom. For this reason, He has gifted us with His most holy scriptures in a book that is called the Bible, which reveals His good, perfect, and acceptable will (Romans 12:2). Within this book, the scriptures reveal God’s character, His holiness, His commands, His purposes, and His design for how believers are to live before Him. Through the teachings of Christ, the instruction of the apostles, the testimony of the prophets, and the unfolding revelation of redemption throughout the Bible, believers come to understand what pleases the Lord and what it means to walk in obedience to Him.

The more a disciple reads, studies, meditates upon, and submits to the Scriptures, the more clearly God’s will becomes known. His Word renews the mind, exposes sin, produces wisdom, strengthens discernment, and guides Believers into truth. The Bible does not merely provide information; it transforms the heart and aligns the believer’s life with the authority and purposes of God.

This is why true discipleship cannot exist apart from the Word of God, the Bible. A disciple learns the will of the King by continually abiding in the words of the King. As believers read, study, meditate upon, and obey the Scriptures, God’s will becomes increasingly visible in their lives, and His Kingdom is made evident through their faithful surrender to Christ. In this way, believers come to understand that God’s Kingdom is not established through human wisdom, power, or effort, but by God’s power, in God’s time, and under God’s authority.

The Cost of True Discipleship

Jesus never softened nor compromised His call to gain followers. In addition, He never lowered the standard to make discipleship easier. Instead, He spoke with sobering clarity:

“Whoever does not bear his cross and come after Me cannot be My disciple.”

Luke 14:27

Before following Him, Jesus taught that a person must first count the cost. Just as a builder carefully considers the expense before beginning construction, a disciple must understand that following Christ requires complete surrender, unwavering allegiance, and a willingness to endure sacrifice for His name’s sake (Luke 14:28–33). Jesus never hid the demands of discipleship, nor did He invite people through promises of worldly comfort, success, or ease. By contrast, He called them to die to self so they might truly live in Him.

A true disciple must:

  • Count the cost before following Christ (Luke 14:28–33)
  • Forsake competing allegiances (Luke 14:33)
  • Love Christ above comfort, family, reputation, and even life itself (Luke 14:26)
  • Take up the cross daily and follow Him (Mark 8:34)

This is not symbolic language or religious exaggeration. The cross represents death—death to self-rule, pride, worldly ambition, and divided allegiance. Every disciple is called to the narrow way, the difficult path of surrender and faithful obedience that leads to life.

The Narrow Gate

Jesus warned:

“Narrow is the gate and difficult is the way which leads to life.”

Matthew 7:14

The gate is narrow because entrance into the Kingdom of God does not come through pride, self-rule, worldly attachment, or divided allegiance. Modern Christianity often seeks comfort without surrender, blessing without obedience, and salvation without discipleship. Yet Jesus never separated salvation from surrender.

To enter the narrow gate, the burdensome baggage of the world must be laid aside—pride, sin, selfish ambition, love of the world, dependence upon self, and every competing loyalty that hinders full devotion to Christ. The narrow gate requires repentance, humility, and a willingness to leave behind the old life to follow the King wholeheartedly.

This way is difficult because true discipleship costs something. It calls believers to walk against the current of the world, endure opposition, deny the desires of the flesh, and remain faithful even through suffering. Yet it is the only path that leads to eternal life.

To follow Christ is to die daily to the rule of self so that Christ may reign fully within us.

How God Builds His Kingdom

The Kingdom of God is built through disciples who live under the authority of Christ. They are not spectators watching from a distance, nor consumers seeking only what benefits them personally. Rather, they are disciples willing to lay down their lives for the sake of Christ—their wants, desires, ambitions, wealth, possessions, reputations, and even their families if necessary. Their allegiance to the King surpasses every earthly attachment.

Scripture declares that believers are:

“A chosen race, a royal priesthood, a holy nation.”

1 Peter 2:9

These words are not accidental; they point directly back to God’s promise to Israel in the Old Testament:

“And you shall be to Me a kingdom of priests and a holy nation.”

Exodus 19:6

What was spoken prophetically in Exodus finds its fulfillment in Christ and in those who belong to Him. Through Jesus, believers are now called out from the world and consecrated unto God as a royal priesthood—a people set apart to represent Him among the nations, proclaim His truth, and live under His authority. Just as the priests of the Old Testament were dedicated wholly unto the service of God, disciples of Christ are now called to lives of holiness, surrender, worship, and faithful obedience before the King.

This fulfilled promise reveals that God’s Kingdom is not built through earthly dominance or worldly systems, but through a consecrated people transformed by Christ and fully devoted to His rule.

Ambassadors of the Kingdom

Our citizenship is in heaven (Philippians 3:20). Though believers live within the kingdoms of this world, their ultimate allegiance belongs to the Kingdom of God. As disciples of Christ, believers are His ambassadors in this world:

“Now then, we are ambassadors for Christ…”

2 Corinthians 5:20

An ambassador represents the authority, message, and interests of the one who sent him. In the same way, disciples of Christ are called to faithfully represent their King before a fallen world. They speak on His behalf, taking every opportunity to defend, testify to, and advocate for the truth and principles of His Kingdom. As Christ’s ambassadors, believers are to reflect His character, values, and authority through lives marked by holiness, truth, love, humility, faithfulness, and obedience.

Christ’s ambassadors do not proclaim their own opinions, personal agendas, or worldly ideologies. Instead, they deliver the message entrusted to them: the gospel of Jesus Christ—the Good News of salvation and reconciliation through Him.

“Go into all the world and preach the gospel…”

Mark 16:15

This calling goes beyond merely speaking words. Christ’s ambassadors are also entrusted with the ministry of reconciliation, inviting others to be restored unto God. They help others understand the gospel, disciple believers in the truth of Scripture, encourage repentance and spiritual restoration, and lead people toward faithful surrender to Jesus Christ.

This connects directly to true discipleship and the building of God’s Kingdom. God advances His Kingdom through surrendered disciples who faithfully represent Christ to others. Wherever believers live under His authority, proclaim His truth, and reflect His character before the world, the reign of the King is made visible.

Living for What Is Eternal

This world and everything within it will pass away because human glory fades and earthly accomplishments crumble. However, every act of faithful obedience done for Christ carries eternal value. The way God builds His Kingdom is when believers:

  • Love Him with undivided devotion
  • Walk in holiness
  • Faithful to His Word, the scriptures
  • Sacrificially love one another
  • Faithfully make disciples
  • Live as citizens of another Kingdom

This is why true discipleship matters because the advancement of Christ’s kingdom does not come by worldly strength, but through surrendered obedience to Jesus Christ. Wherever hearts submit to the King, the Kingdom is revealed, and lives are transformed.

Why Allegiance to Christ Cannot Be Shared

Jesus said:

“No one can serve two masters.”

Matthew 6:24

Shared allegiance is impossible because Christ does not accept partial devotion or selective obedience. Instead, He calls for the whole heart, the whole life, and the whole will.

Throughout history, some religious groups have interpreted separation from the world to mean complete withdrawal from political involvement, military service, voting, or civic participation. Yet many of these same groups will strongly defend their own freedoms and rights when threatened, even while refusing involvement in defending the rights, welfare, or protection of others within the nation in which they live. Scripture, however, presents believers not as isolated spectators removed from society, but as ambassadors of Christ living faithfully within the world while remaining ultimately devoted to the Kingdom of God.

Having allegiance to one’s country has its proper place. Jesus called His followers “the salt of the earth” and “the light of the world” (Matthew 5:13–16). As Christ’s ambassadors, believers should desire justice, righteousness, mercy, peace, and the protection of human dignity within society. Advocating for what is right, defending the oppressed, caring for the less fortunate, and encouraging leaders toward laws and policies that uphold justice and moral good can all reflect the believer’s responsibility to be a godly influence within the world. In this sense, faithful citizenship and responsible civic involvement can serve as expressions of loving one’s neighbor and seeking the good of others.

However, no earthly allegiance may ever replace or compete with allegiance to Christ. Political identity, national loyalty, personal ideology, and worldly causes must always remain subject to the authority of the King. Whenever devotion to country, party, ideology, or personal interest begins to override obedience to Scripture, the heart has crossed into divided allegiance.

Scripture leaves no neutral ground:

“He who is not with Me is against Me.”

Matthew 12:30

To claim Christ while clinging to the values, priorities, and ambitions of the world is to misunderstand both the Kingdom and the King. The cross leaves no room for divided loyalty. True discipleship begins where self-rule ends and where Christ alone reigns supreme over every allegiance, every desire, and every area of life.

Examine Yourself

Scripture commands believers:

“Examine yourselves as to whether you are in the faith.”

2 Corinthians 13:5

The question is not merely whether we profess Christ, but whether our lives reveal surrendered allegiance to Him. Each believer must honestly look within and ask difficult but necessary questions: Have we truly submitted to Christ’s authority, or have we merely added Jesus to our existing plans and personal ambitions? Have we obeyed His commands wholeheartedly, or have we embraced only the parts of discipleship that feel comfortable, convenient, or beneficial to ourselves? Are we living according to the will of the King, or are we still clinging to control over our own lives?

Jesus gave a sobering warning:

“Not everyone who says to Me, ‘Lord, Lord,’ shall enter the kingdom of heaven, but he who does the will of My Father.”

Matthew 7:21

These words remind us that genuine discipleship is revealed not merely through verbal confession, religious activity, or outward association with Christianity, but through faithful obedience and surrendered living. This examination is not about achieving sinless perfection, but about the direction and posture of the heart. A true disciple is not sinless, but surrendered; not flawless, but faithful. Though believers still struggle against sin and weakness, they desire to follow Christ, obey His Word, repent when they fail, and continue growing in holiness and submission to the will of God.

Where allegiance is genuine, obedience follows.

A Call to Surrender

The call of Christ has never changed, given that He does not call us to admire Him from a distance, but rather He calls us to follow Him.

This surrender is not loss—it is life.

Jesus promised:

“Whoever loses his life for My sake will find it.”

Matthew 16:25

The cost of discipleship is real, but so is the promise of eternal life in Christ. If faith has grown comfortable, obedience delayed, or allegiance divided, the invitation still stands:

Return to Christ and submit to His authority. Then take up your cross and follow Him daily.

This is not condemnation—it is grace.

The narrow way still leads to life, and the call to discipleship still stands because God is still building His Kingdom through those who follow the King.

The Call Is Clear

If we claim the name of Christ, we must live as His disciples and ambassadors to:

  • Love the Lord with undivided devotion (Matthew 22:37)
  • Love one another as Christ commanded (John 13:34–35)
  • Present your life as a living sacrifice (Romans 12:1–2)
  • Walk in holiness (1 Peter 1:15–16)
  • Be faithful stewards (1 Corinthians 4:2)
  • Including, to go and make disciples (Matthew 28:19–20)

These are not optional for disciples—this is the mandate of the Kingdom. Believers are called to stand firm in the truth, speak faithfully on behalf of Christ, and live lives fully surrendered to His authority. Rather than spending our lives constructing earthly platforms, personal kingdoms, and monuments to ourselves that will eventually corrode, crumble, and turn to dust, we are called to labor for something eternal. Christ commanded His followers to build His Kingdom by making disciples of all nations—transforming lives through the gospel, teaching obedience to His Word, and leading others into faithful surrender to the King. Earthly accomplishments fade away, but the work of God’s Kingdom endures forever.

A Prayer of Surrender

Heavenly Father,

Teach us to deny ourselves, take up our cross daily, and follow Christ with undivided hearts. Help us to surrender every competing allegiance and to live faithfully as ambassadors of Your Kingdom in this world. Give us wisdom through Your Word, courage to stand for truth, and humility to walk in obedience to Your will. May our lives reflect the character of Christ as we seek to make disciples and build what is eternal. Strengthen us to remain faithful until the day Your Kingdom is fully revealed in glory.

In Jesus’ name, Amen.
